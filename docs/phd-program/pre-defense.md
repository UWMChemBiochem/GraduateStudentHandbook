# Pre-Defense

Before completing the dissertation, convene your doctoral committee for a preliminary thesis review, or “pre-defense,” which also serves as a milestones meeting.

!!! info
	This review must take place no later than one month before the dissertation defense. It is strongly recommended that it be held about six months in advance to allow time for revisions.

The pre-defense is intended to help you organize the major points of the dissertation, confirm the overall structure, and identify any remaining laboratory work needed for completion. It is not an examination or a dress rehearsal, but a review designed to reduce major revisions before the dissertation defense.

The required abstract should be concise and include the proposed chapter headings. It should summarize your progress and planned final experiments rather than serve as a full manuscript.
