# Ph.D. Program Timeline and Milestones

## Semester 1

!!! info
	During this semester, you should take three [entrance exams](../general-departmental-policies/entrance-exams.md) and complete [three rotations](../general-departmental-policies/selecting-major-professor.md).

The Graduate Advising Subcommittee will determine your [first-semester courses](../general-departmental-policies/first-semester-advising.md). Typically, this will include:

- Two [graduate courses](../coursework/index.md) (6 credits).
- [CHEM 99X (Research)](../coursework/research.md), 2 credits per rotation (6 credits total).
- [CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar)](../coursework/graduate-seminar.md), 1 credit, taken as audit.

You may register for an additional graduate course with a corresponding reduction in research credits. However, this option is generally discouraged, as students will also need to devote a substantial amount of time to rotations and TA duties.

!!! success "Milestone"
	By the end of the semester, you must have [selected a major professor](../general-departmental-policies/selecting-major-professor.md).

## Semester 2

!!! info
	Ph.D. students must retake any failed entrance exams each time they are offered until all required exams are passed.

The major professor will determine your courses for this and subsequent semesters. Typically, this will include:

- Two graduate courses (6 credits).
- [CHEM 93X (Advanced Seminar)](../coursework/advanced-seminar.md), 1 credit.
- CHEM 99X (Research), 5 credits.
- CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar), 1 credit, taken as audit.

You are expected to move into your new office space and formally begin your research project. The summer is your first opportunity to focus almost entirely on research, and you are expected to make progress during this period.

!!! success "Milestone"
	In consultation with your major professor, you will form your [doctoral committee](doctoral-committee) and schedule your first [milestones meeting](../general-departmental-policies/milestones-meetings.md), which must take place before the third semester.

!!! warning
	Avoid scheduling the meeting during the summer, when it may be difficult to convene the committee.

!!! success "Milestone"
	If your second semester is in the spring, you will also present your first [poster at the Chemistry Research Symposium](../general-departmental-policies/chemistry-research-symposium-presentation.md); otherwise, you will present it the following semester.

## Semester 3

!!! success "Milestone"
	Before the start of your third semester, you must have passed the required entrance exams.

!!! success "Milestone"
	You will present your [CHEM 912 seminar](../coursework/graduate-seminar.md) in the third or fourth semester.

Typically, you will register for:

- Up to two graduate courses (6 credits).
- CHEM 93X (Advanced Seminar), 1 credit.
- CHEM 99X (Research), 5 credits; 4 credits if presenting in CHEM 912.
- CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar), 1 credit, taken as audit (**graded if you are presenting**).

If you take only one graduate course this semester, increase your research credits accordingly.

## Semester 4

Typically, you will register for:

- CHEM 93X (Advanced Seminar), 1 credit.
- CHEM 99X (Research), 9 credits.
- CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar), 1 credit, taken as audit (**graded if you are presenting**).
 
Any remaining graduate courses should be completed this semester. If you take any courses, adjust your research credits so your total is 12 credits, not including audit credits. 

!!! success "Milestone"
	You will hold your [preliminary examination](preliminary-exam.md), which also serves as your second milestones meeting.

!!! warning
	Avoid scheduling it during the summer, when it may be difficult to convene the committee.

!!! success "Milestone"
	You will also present your second poster at the Chemistry Research Symposium this semester or the following semester.

## Semesters 5 to 9+

Having passed the preliminary exam, you are now a dissertator and must enroll in exactly 3 credits each semester:

- CHEM 93X (Advanced Seminar), 1 credit.
- CHEM 99X (Research), 2 credits.
- CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar), 1 credit, taken as audit.

Dissertators may request a dissertator credit overload if a course is offered that was not available during their coursework period but is beneficial or essential to their research, dissertation, or career objectives. To begin the process, contact {{ gradCoord }} and submit a [Request for Exception form]({{ URLGradSchoolForms }}).

!!! success "Milestones"
	You will hold your annual milestones meetings and present a poster at each Chemistry Research Symposium.

!!! info
	About six months, and no later than one month, before the dissertation defense, you should hold the [pre-defense](pre-defense.md), which also serves as a milestone meeting.

## Final Semester

As usual, you will register for:

- CHEM 93X (Advanced Seminar), 1 credit.
- CHEM 99X (Research), 2 credits.
- CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar), 1 credit, taken as audit.

You should plan to spend most of this semester writing your dissertation. Because this process often takes more time than expected, plan accordingly! 

!!! info
	You must provide a copy of your dissertation to your doctoral committee at least 14 days before your [dissertation defense](completing-the-phd.md). 

The dissertation must follow the Graduate School's [thesis formatting guidelines]({{ URLThesisFormat }}). 

The Graduate School’s [Dissertation Basecamp]({{ URLBasecamp }}) provides the resources you need for the journey of your dissertation and offers access to a [3-day Dissertation Writing Retreat]({{ URLWritingRetreat }}) and Writing groups.
