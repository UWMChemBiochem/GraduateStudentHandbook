# Preliminary Exam

The preliminary examination should be completed by the end of your second year. 

Follow the steps on the [Graduate School website]({{ URLMilestones }}) to schedule the preliminary exam and submit applications for:

1. Preliminary Examination.
2. Proposal Hearing.
3. Dissertator Status.

The preliminary exam and proposal hearing are the same event for the Ph.D. in Chemistry. However, you **must** apply for both milestones individually and both should be completed at the same time.

The preliminary exam has two parts: 

1. A 30-minute literature discussion. 
2. A research presentation of up to 1 hour. 

The total examination time must not exceed 2 hours.

The major professor will select a paper for the literature portion and provide it four weeks before the exam. During the first part of the exam, you will briefly present the main results, critically evaluate the content and findings, and answer questions about the paper.

The second part of the exam is based on the student’s research progress and dissertation plans. The student must have a well-defined research problem and substantial preliminary results before convening the committee. 

!!! warning
	The exam may be repeated at the committee’s discretion, but it must be completed by the end of the second year to remain in good academic standing.
