# TA Training and Orientation

Students who accept a TA appointment must attend both departmental and university-wide TA orientation programs. These sessions introduce teaching duties, expectations, safety responsibilities, and the structure of undergraduate instruction in the department. The final portion of the program includes meetings with the faculty members for whom students will teach during their first semester.

International students must also attend the international TA orientation. International TAs who do not meet the [required English speaking score]({{ URLMITAAScores }}) must pass the [MITAA language test]({{ URLMITAA }}) before the start of classes. This requirement is in addition to the [language requirements for regular admission]({{ URLEnglishProficiency }}).

!!! note
	TA support is available only to students in good academic standing.

Failure to follow department work rules, instructor directives, or laboratory safety requirements results in disciplinary action, including

- verbal reprimand.
- written reprimand.
- suspension without pay. 
- dismissal. 

Repeated or serious violations will affect future teaching assignments and funding.

Verbal and written reprimands may be imposed by the course instructor following an investigatory meeting. The student may file a grievance with the Undergraduate/Appeals Subcommittee within 10 working days of the reprimand.
