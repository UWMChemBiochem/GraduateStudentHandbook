# Entrance Exams

All entering graduate students must complete at least two entrance examinations:

- One divisional exam in your intended discipline (analytical, inorganic, organic, physical chemistry, or biochemistry).
- One additional exam from another discipline, subject to divisional rules.

The second exam is chosen by the student unless it is assigned by the divisional faculty. Each division sets its own passing scores. In absence of divisional passing scores, a score at or above the 50th percentile level is required to pass an entrance exam.

Students are encouraged to take three entrance exams during orientation week to improve the chances of passing at least two.

!!! note
	For M.S. students, the entrance exams are used for advising only. 

!!! warning
	Ph.D. students may retake failed entrance exams multiple times. However, they must pass them by the start of the third semester to remain in good academic standing. 

Entrance exams are typically offered three times per year, at the beginning of the fall, spring, and summer terms. The exact dates will be announced by {{ gradCoord }}.
