# Safety and Security

Safety is a primary responsibility in all research and teaching activities. Students must follow the Chemical Hygiene Plan and the ACS publication [“Safety in Academic Chemistry Laboratories”]({{ URLACSSafety }}). Questions should be directed to the Safety Subcommittee or the major professor. All students must attend an annual safety seminar.

Students are also responsible for protecting department facilities, equipment, and data. Offices and laboratories should be locked when unattended, and computers and laptops should be secured.

Graduate students have keycard access to conduct research during the evenings and weekends. While enjoying the privilege of 24-hour access, it is your responsibility to ensure the building’s security. 

Students are encouraged to use the [free and safe ride service]({{ URLBOSS }}) when working late at night. 

Emergency contact numbers to [UWM Police]({{ URLUWMPolice }}) are 

- 9-911 from a campus phone.
- (414) 229-9911 from a cell phone. 
- For non-emergencies, call (414) 229-4627.
