# Milestones Meetings

Students are expected to select a major professor by the end of their first semester. By the conclusion of the second semester, the student and major professor must establish one of the following committees: 

- [ Doctoral committee](../phd-program/doctoral-committee.md) (Ph.D. program).
- [ Thesis committee](../ms-program/thesis-option.md) (M.S. program, Thesis Option).
- [ Capstone committee](../ms-program/non-thesis-option.md) (M.S. program, Non-thesis Option). 

All graduate students must meet annually with their committee. 
These **milestones meetings** evaluate each student’s progress in coursework, teaching, mastery of subject knowledge, and research/capstone experience, and ensure timely advancement toward degree completion.

The initial milestones meeting must be held before the start of the third semester, ideally during the second semester. This meeting includes a review of the student’s coursework and teaching activities during the first two semesters, as well as an introduction to the proposed research topic or capstone experience.

Following the initial meeting, milestones meetings must be held within 12, 24, 36, etc. months of the _initial_ meeting. During each meeting, the student presents research progress, reviews results, and outlines plans for future work in consultation with the committee.

!!! info
	Students are responsible for scheduling and convening their committee for each required milestones meeting.

!!! warning
	Failure to hold a milestones meeting within the required timeframe will result in loss of good academic standing. 

Students who do not make satisfactory progress will be placed on probation and required to hold a follow-up meeting with their committee within 6 months.
