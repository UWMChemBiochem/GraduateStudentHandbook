# Selecting a Major Professor

!!! note
	This section does not apply to M.S. students pursuing the Non-thesis Option.

Choosing a major professor is one of the most important decisions in graduate study.

During orientation week, students attend faculty research talks to learn about the research areas available in the department. After those talks, students submit a form listing three research groups in which they would like to rotate during the first semester.

Each rotation lasts four weeks. The exact format of the rotation is at the discretion of the faculty member. For instance, it could involve attending group meetings, conducting a limited research project, or studying the relevant literature.

!!! info
	Contact the faculty member before each rotation to establish the requirements for satisfactory completion.

At the end of each rotation, the faculty member signs your rotation form to attest to satisfactory completion of the rotation. 

When all rotations are completed, students submit another form listing their two preferred research groups in order of preference.

!!! note
	These choices do not necessarily have to be one of the three groups that you were doing rotations with.

The department reviews student preferences and assigns students to research groups based on student choice, group capacity, and funding. Assignment to a first-choice group is not guaranteed. Failure to complete the rotation process may affect TA support, which the major professor requests each semester on behalf of the student.

All forms will be distributed by {{ gradCoord }} and should be returned to her.
