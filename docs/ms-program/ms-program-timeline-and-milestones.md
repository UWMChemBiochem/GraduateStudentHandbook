# M.S. Program Timeline and Milestones

## Semester 1

!!! info
	During this semester, you should take three [entrance exams](../general-departmental-policies/entrance-exams.md).

M.S. students pursuing the Thesis Option must complete [three rotations](../general-departmental-policies/selecting-major-professor.md). Students in the Non-thesis Option do not complete rotations since they were assigned a major professor at admission.

The Graduate Advising Subcommittee will determine your [first-semester courses](../general-departmental-policies/first-semester-advising.md). Typically, this will include:

- Two [graduate courses](../coursework/index.md) (6 credits).
- [CHEM 99X (Research)](../coursework/research.md), 6 credits; 2 credits per rotation for students completing rotations.
- [CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar)](../coursework/graduate-seminar.md), 1 credit, taken as audit.

You may register for an additional graduate course with a corresponding reduction in research credits. However, this option is generally discouraged, as students will also need to devote a substantial amount of time to rotations (if applicable) and TA duties.

!!! success "Milestone"
	M.S. students pursuing the Thesis Option must have [selected a major professor](../general-departmental-policies/selecting-major-professor.md) by the end of the semester.

## Semester 2

The major professor will determine your courses for this and subsequent semesters. Typically, this will include:

- Two graduate courses (6 credits).
- [CHEM 93X (Advanced Seminar)](../coursework/advanced-seminar.md), 1 credit.
- CHEM 99X (Research), 5 credits.
- CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar), 1 credit, taken as audit.

You are expected to move into your new office space and formally begin your research project or capstone experience. The summer is your first opportunity to focus almost entirely on this work, and you are expected to make progress during this period.

!!! success "Milestone"
	In consultation with your major professor, you will form your [thesis committee](thesis-option.md) or capstone committee and schedule your first [milestones meeting](../general-departmental-policies/milestones-meetings.md), which must take place before the third semester.

!!! warning
	Avoid scheduling the meeting during the summer, when it may be difficult to convene the committee.

!!! success "Milestone"
	If your second semester is in the spring, you will also present your first [poster at the Chemistry Research Symposium](../general-departmental-policies/chemistry-research-symposium-presentation.md); otherwise, you will present it the following semester.

## Semester 3

!!! success "Milestone"
	You will present your [CHEM 912 seminar](../coursework/graduate-seminar.md) in the third or fourth semester.

Typically, you will register for:

- The remaining graduate course (3 credits).
- CHEM 93X (Advanced Seminar), 1 credit.
- CHEM 99X (Research), 8 credits; 7 credits if presenting in CHEM 912.
- CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar), 1 credit, taken as audit (**graded if you are presenting**).

## Semester 4

Typically, you will register for:

- CHEM 93X (Advanced Seminar), 1 credit.
- CHEM 99X (Research), at least 5 credits; at least 4 credits if presenting in CHEM 912.
- CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar), 1 credit, taken as audit (**graded if you are presenting**).

!!! success "Milestones"
	You will hold your annual milestones meetings and present a poster at each Chemistry Research Symposium.
 
## Semester 5+

Typically, you will register for:

- CHEM 93X (Advanced Seminar), 1 credit.
- CHEM 99X (Research), at least 5 credits.
- CHEM 912 (Graduate Seminar), 1 credit, taken as audit.

Students pursuing the Thesis Option should plan to spend the final semester writing their thesis. Because this process often takes more time than expected, plan accordingly! 

!!! info
	You must provide a copy of your thesis to your thesis committee at least 7 days before your [thesis defense](completing-the-ms.md). 

The thesis must follow the Graduate School's [thesis formatting guidelines]({{ URLThesisFormat }}).
