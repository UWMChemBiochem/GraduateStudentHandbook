# Core Courses

## Analytical Chemistry

- CHEM 724 (Electrochemistry).
- CHEM 726 (Separations).
- CHEM 726 (Special Topics).

## Biochemistry

- CHEM 601G (Protein Structure and Function).
- CHEM 602G (Cellular Processes).
- CHEM 604G (Metabolism).

If a core course was already taken as an undergraduate, substitutions such as CHEM 701, CHEM 781, CHEM 614G, or CHEM 741 may be approved.

## Chemical Education

- CHEM 731 (Topics in Chemistry Education Research).
- ED PSY 624G (Educational Statistical Methods I). 
- ED PSY 631G (Cognition: Learning, Problem Solving and Thinking).

ED PSY 724 (Educational Statistical Methods II) is also recommended. 
## Inorganic Chemistry

- CHEM 611G (Physical Inorganic Chemistry). 
- CHEM 612G (Transition Metal Chemistry).
- One graduate-level physical chemistry or physics course chosen with the major professor.

## Organic Chemistry

- CHEM 640G (Advanced Survey of Organic Chemistry) or previous equivalent third semester courses in advanced organic chemistry.

Any two of the following courses:

- CHEM 740 (Advanced Organic Chemistry Methods in Synthetic Chemistry).
- CHEM 741 (Special Topics, such as Medicinal Chemistry,  Organometallic Chemistry, Nanomaterials, Biomolecular Recognition).
- CHEM 748 (Physical Organic Chemistry).
- CHEM 647G (Physical Methods of Organic Chemistry).
- Other, more topical courses.

## Physical Chemistry

The Physical Chemistry division requires four core courses:

- CHEM 662G (Chemical Kinetics and Reaction Dynamics).
- CHEM 762 (Computer Modeling in Chemistry).
- CHEM 765 (Statistical Thermodynamics).
- CHEM 767 (Basic Quantum Chemistry).

The fourth course replaces one of the minor courses.
