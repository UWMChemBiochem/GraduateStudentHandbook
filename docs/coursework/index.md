# Overview

This chapter summarizes the course requirements for the Ph.D. and M.S. programs; unless otherwise noted, the requirements apply to both programs. 

Course selection is determined by the major professor.

!!! warning
	Students who register for courses other than the approved courses will lose their good academic standing.

The departmental Ph.D. coursework requirement consists of 18 credits of graduate-level coursework at the 600 level or above. For 600-level courses, students must enroll in the section marked with a “G” to receive graduate credit. 500-level courses do not count towards the requirement, even if marked with a “G”. Ph.D. students typically take six 3-credit courses.

The departmental M.S. coursework requirement consists of 15 credits of graduate-level coursework at the 600 level or above. An M.S. student typically takes five 3-credit courses.

The goal is to build a program that supports the student’s research project and professional development.
The student must take all [ core courses required by their discipline](core-courses.md).
The other courses can be taken in one or more minor areas.

If a student has already completed a required core course as an undergraduate, the major professor and committee should identify an appropriate substitute.
